Lailatul Isra Wal Mi Raj

On Thursday, April 18, 1985, Muslims observed Lailatul Isra wal Mi‘raj, marking the 27th of Rajab in the Islamic lunar calendar. This sacred night commemorates the Prophet Muhammad’s extraordinary journey—from Mecca to Jerusalem (Isra), followed by his ascent into the heavens (Mi‘raj)—and is one of the most significant observances in the Islamic calendar. Although the exact date of the original event remains a matter of theological discourse, the 27th of Rajab is the widely accepted date for its commemoration. Communities often observe the night with special prayers, Qur’an recitation, and reflections on the spiritual significance of the journey.
