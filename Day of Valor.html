Day of Valor

Araw ng Kagitingan, also known as Day of Valor, honors the bravery of Filipino and American soldiers during World War II, especially in the Battle of Bataan. It remembers the sacrifices made during the infamous Bataan Death March in 1942. The day serves as a tribute to courage, resilience, and patriotism. Ceremonies are held at Mt. Samat Shrine and other memorial sites.
